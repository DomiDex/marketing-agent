# /competitor-research

Research and analyze competitors for strategic insights.

## Overview

**What it does:** Provides frameworks for competitive intelligence—including competitor profiles, market landscape analysis, battlecards, win/loss analysis, and ongoing monitoring.

**When to use it:**
- Understanding competitive landscape
- Creating sales battlecards
- Analyzing competitor strategies
- Preparing win/loss analysis
- Positioning against alternatives

**What you'll get:**
- Structured competitor analysis
- Comparison frameworks
- Strategic recommendations
- Monitoring approach
- Actionable insights

---

## How to Invoke

**Command:** `/competitor-research`

**Trigger phrases:**
- "Analyze competitor..."
- "Competitive analysis of..."
- "Competitor research on..."
- "Create a battlecard..."
- "How does X compare to Y..."

## Tips for Best Results

### Do This
- **Be specific about competitors** — Name them explicitly
- **Clarify the goal** — Sales battlecard vs strategic analysis
- **Share your differentiators** — Helps position comparison
- **Include your target market** — Filters relevant competitors

### Avoid This
- **Analyzing too many at once** — Focus on 3-5 key competitors
- **One-time analysis** — Competitive landscape changes
- **Dismissing competitors** — Respect what they do well
- **Copying competitors** — Learn from them, differentiate from them

### Pro Tips
1. **Talk to customers** — Best competitive intel comes from them
2. **Sign up for competitors** — Experience their product firsthand
3. **Monitor their content** — Subscribe to blogs/newsletters
4. **Check review sites** — G2, Capterra reveal real sentiment

### Battlecard Template
- 30-second overview
- Feature comparison
- Landmines (questions that favor you)
- Objection handling
- Proof points
