# Sales Enablement

## Before Creating Collateral

Gather essential context:

### 1. Sales Motion
- Inbound or outbound?
- Deal size and sales cycle length
- Who's involved in the buying process?
- What's the typical close rate?

### 2. Buyer Understanding
- Who are you targeting? (title, role)
- What do they care about?
- What problems are they trying to solve?
- What alternatives are they considering?

### 3. Product/Offering
- What are you selling?
- Key features and benefits
- Pricing model
- Competitive advantages

### 4. Competitive Landscape
- Who do you compete against?
- How do deals typically get lost?
- What objections come up?
- What do competitors claim?

### 5. Existing Materials
- What collateral exists already?
- What's working or not working?
- Are there brand guidelines?
- What proof points are available?

## Collateral Types

### 1. One-Pagers / Product Sheets

**Purpose:** Quick reference for prospects and sales
**When Used:** After initial interest, before deep dive
**Length:** One page (PDF/print-friendly)

**Structure:**
```
Header: Logo + Product Name + Tagline

Hero: Primary value proposition (1-2 sentences)

Key Benefits (3-4):
- Benefit 1 with supporting point
- Benefit 2 with supporting point
- Benefit 3 with supporting point

How It Works (3 steps or visual)

Social Proof:
- Key metric or stat
- Customer logo bar
- Brief testimonial

Differentiators (vs. alternatives)

CTA + Contact Info
```

### 2. Battlecards

**Purpose:** Win competitive deals
**When Used:** When competitor comes up in sales process
**Length:** 1-2 pages, scannable

**Structure:**
```
Competitor Overview:
- Who they are
- Their positioning
- Their typical customer

Quick Comparison Table:
[Feature/Capability comparison]

Why We Win:
- Key differentiators
- Where we're stronger
- Customer proof points

Why We Lose:
- Their strengths
- Where they're stronger
- How to mitigate

Common Objections:
- Objection ‚Üí Response
- Objection ‚Üí Response

Landmines to Plant:
- Questions that expose weaknesses

Customer Stories:
- Customers who switched from them
```

### 3. Cold Email Sequences

**Purpose:** Generate meetings/interest
**When Used:** Outbound prospecting
**Length:** 3-5 emails over 2-3 weeks

**Principles:**
- Personalization is mandatory
- Value before pitch
- Clear, single CTA
- Short (under 100 words ideal)

### 4. Sales Deck Outlines

**Purpose:** Structure discovery and demo calls
**When Used:** First meetings, demos, proposals
**Length:** 10-15 slides typical

**Structure:**
```
1. Title + Agenda
2. Problem/Challenge
3. Cost of Problem
4. Solution Overview
5. How It Works
6. Key Differentiators
7. Social Proof / Case Studies
8. Results Customers See
9. Pricing/Packaging (if appropriate)
10. Implementation/Timeline
11. Next Steps
```

### 5. Objection Handling Scripts

**Purpose:** Prepare reps for common pushback
**When Used:** Training, reference during calls
**Format:** Objection ‚Üí Acknowledge ‚Üí Bridge ‚Üí Answer

## One-Pager Framework

### Above the Fold

**Logo Bar:** Your logo (upper left)

**Headline:** Primary value proposition
- Lead with outcome, not feature
- Specific to target audience
- Under 10 words

**Subheadline:** Supporting detail
- Expand on the how
- 1-2 sentences max

### Key Benefits Section

3-4 benefits, each with:
- **Benefit header** (outcome-focused)
- Supporting sentence with specifics
- Icon or visual if appropriate

**Example:**
```
üöÄ Ship 3x Faster
Automated testing and deployment means your team
ships features in days, not weeks.
```

### How It Works

3-step visual showing:
1. Input/trigger
2. Process/magic
3. Output/result

Keep simple. Reduce complexity.

### Social Proof

Options:
- **Stat:** "2,000+ teams trust [Product]"
- **Logos:** Customer logo bar (5-8 logos)
- **Quote:** One powerful testimonial
- **Metric:** Customer result ("47% faster deployments")

### Bottom Section

**Differentiators:**
Quick list of what makes you different
- Unlike X, we do Y
- Only [Product] offers Z

**CTA:**
- Clear next step
- Contact information
- QR code to landing page (optional)

## Cold Email Framework

### Email 1: The Hook

**Subject lines (test multiple):**
- Question about [their challenge]
- [Mutual connection] suggested I reach out
- Quick thought on [specific observation]

**Body structure:**
```
[Personalized opener - specific to them]

[Pain point you solve]

[Social proof - quick credibility]

[Soft CTA - low commitment]

[Signature]
```

**Length:** 50-80 words
**CTA:** Suggest a call, ask a question

### Email 2: The Value Add

**Subject:** Re: [original subject] or new angle

**Body structure:**
```
[Acknowledge no response - briefly]

[Provide value - insight, resource, observation]

[Reframe the problem/solution]

[Slightly more direct CTA]
```

**Length:** 60-100 words
**CTA:** More specific ask

### Email 3: The Case Study

**Subject:** How [similar company] solved [problem]

**Body structure:**
```
[Brief context]

[Relevant case study - specific results]

[Connection to their situation]

[CTA - offer to share more detail]
```

**Length:** 75-100 words
**CTA:** Share more detail, quick call

### Email 4: The Breakup

**Subject:** Should I close your file?

**Body structure:**
```
[Acknowledge you've reached out]

[Final value statement]

[Easy out - if timing is wrong]

[Door open for future]
```

**Length:** 40-60 words
**CTA:** Simple yes/no or redirect

## Objection Handling Framework

### The LAER Model

**Listen:** Fully hear the objection
**Acknowledge:** Show you understand
**Explore:** Ask questions to understand root cause
**Respond:** Address with value

### Common Objection Categories

**Price Objections:**
- "It's too expensive"
- "We don't have the budget"
- "Competitor is cheaper"

**Timing Objections:**
- "Not a priority right now"
- "We're too busy"
- "Let's revisit next quarter"

**Authority Objections:**
- "I need to check with my boss"
- "It's not my decision"
- "Other stakeholders need to weigh in"

**Need Objections:**
- "We're fine with our current solution"
- "We don't really need this"
- "Not sure it applies to us"

**Trust Objections:**
- "You're too small/new"
- "We've never heard of you"
- "How do we know it works?"

### Objection Response Template

```markdown
## Objection: "[Exact objection]"

### Why They Say This
[Root cause or underlying concern]

### Response Framework
1. **Acknowledge:** "[Show empathy statement]"
2. **Clarify:** "[Question to understand more]"
3. **Address:** "[Response with value]"
4. **Confirm:** "[Check if resolved]"

### Example Response
"[Word-for-word example response]"

### Proof Point to Share
[Case study, stat, or reference]
```

## Sales Deck Structure

### Opening (Slides 1-2)
- Title + your logo
- Agenda (set expectations)

### Problem (Slides 3-4)
- The challenge they face
- Cost/impact of the problem
- Why it's getting worse

### Solution (Slides 5-7)
- Your approach (high level)
- How it works (3-step visual)
- Key differentiators

### Proof (Slides 8-10)
- Customer case studies
- Results and metrics
- Social proof (logos, quotes)

### Path Forward (Slides 11-12)
- Implementation overview
- Timeline
- Pricing (if appropriate)
- Next steps

### Appendix
- Detailed features
- Technical specs
- Additional case studies
- FAQ

## Personalization Best Practices

### Research Checklist

Before outreach, find:
- Recent company news
- Role-specific challenges
- Mutual connections
- Content they've shared
- Company tech stack
- Recent job changes

### Personalization Levels

**Level 1: Basic**
- Company name
- Person's name
- Industry reference

**Level 2: Relevant**
- Role-specific pain point
- Company-specific challenge
- Industry trend

**Level 3: Deep**
- Reference their content
- Mention mutual connection
- Specific observation about their company
- Recent news or trigger event
