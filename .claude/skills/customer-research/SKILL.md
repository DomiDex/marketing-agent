# Customer Research

## Before Creating Research Materials

Gather essential context:

### 1. Research Objectives
- What decisions will this research inform?
- What hypotheses are you testing?
- What do you already know vs. need to learn?
- How will findings be used?

### 2. Target Audience
- Who are you researching? (customers, prospects, churned users)
- What segments matter most?
- How will you recruit participants?
- How many responses/interviews do you need?

### 3. Current Knowledge
- What do you already know about customers?
- What data do you have? (analytics, support tickets, sales calls)
- What assumptions need validation?
- What previous research exists?

### 4. Constraints
- Timeline for research
- Budget for incentives
- Access to customers
- Internal resources

## Research Types

### 1. Customer Interviews

**Best For:**
- Deep qualitative insights
- Understanding motivations and emotions
- Exploring "why" behind behaviors
- Discovering unexpected insights

**Sample Size:** 5-15 interviews per segment
**Duration:** 30-60 minutes each

### 2. Surveys

**Best For:**
- Quantitative validation
- Measuring at scale
- Tracking trends over time
- Segmentation analysis

**Sample Size:** 100+ responses for statistical significance
**Duration:** 5-15 minutes to complete

### 3. Customer Personas

**Best For:**
- Aligning teams on target customer
- Guiding content and messaging
- Informing product decisions
- Sales enablement

### 4. Jobs-to-be-Done Analysis

**Best For:**
- Understanding core motivations
- Finding new market opportunities
- Positioning and messaging
- Product development priorities

### 5. Voice-of-Customer Analysis

**Best For:**
- Mining existing feedback for insights
- Identifying patterns in complaints
- Finding messaging opportunities
- Prioritizing improvements

## Interview Script Framework

### Structure Overview

```
1. Introduction & Warm-up (5 min)
2. Background & Context (10 min)
3. Problem Exploration (15 min)
4. Solution Evaluation (15 min)
5. Outcomes & Impact (10 min)
6. Wrap-up (5 min)
```

### Section 1: Introduction & Warm-up

**Goals:** Build rapport, set expectations, get consent

**Script:**
```
"Thanks so much for taking the time to chat. I'm [Name] from [Company].

Before we start, I want to explain what we're doing. We're trying to
understand how [target audience] approach [problem area]. There are no
right or wrong answers - we just want to learn from your experience.

This will take about [X] minutes. Everything you share is confidential
and will only be used to improve our product/service.

Do you mind if I record this for my notes? The recording won't be
shared outside our team.

Do you have any questions before we begin?"
```

### Section 2: Background & Context

**Goals:** Understand their role, company, and relevance

**Questions:**
- "Tell me about your role. What are you responsible for?"
- "How long have you been in this role/at this company?"
- "What does a typical day/week look like for you?"
- "Who do you work most closely with?"

### Section 3: Problem Exploration

**Goals:** Deep understanding of the problem and its impact

**Questions:**
- "Walk me through how you currently handle [problem area]."
- "What's the most frustrating part of that process?"
- "When did you first realize this was a problem?"
- "What happens when this doesn't go well?"
- "How much time/money does this cost you?"
- "What have you tried before to solve this?"
- "Why didn't those solutions work?"

**Probing Techniques:**
- "Can you give me a specific example?"
- "Tell me more about that."
- "Why do you think that is?"
- "How did that make you feel?"
- "What happened next?"

### Section 4: Solution Evaluation

**Goals:** Understand how they evaluate and choose solutions

**For Prospects:**
- "What would an ideal solution look like?"
- "What's most important when choosing a tool for this?"
- "What would make you hesitant to try something new?"
- "Who else would need to be involved in the decision?"

**For Customers:**
- "What made you decide to try [your product]?"
- "What did you expect vs. what actually happened?"
- "What do you like most/least about it?"
- "If you could change one thing, what would it be?"

**For Churned Users:**
- "Walk me through your decision to stop using [product]."
- "What were the warning signs before you left?"
- "What would have kept you as a customer?"
- "What are you using now instead?"

### Section 5: Outcomes & Impact

**Goals:** Quantify value and identify success criteria

**Questions:**
- "How do you measure success in [area]?"
- "What results have you seen since [change/using product]?"
- "What's different now compared to before?"
- "How has this affected your team/company?"

### Section 6: Wrap-up

**Goals:** Final insights, referrals, thank them

**Script:**
```
"We're almost at time. A few quick final questions:

- Is there anything I should have asked but didn't?
- What would you tell someone in your position who's
  facing similar challenges?
- Do you know anyone else who might be willing to chat
  with us about this?

Thank you so much for your time. This was incredibly helpful.
[Mention incentive/follow-up if applicable]"
```

## Survey Design Framework

### Survey Structure

```
1. Screener questions (if needed)
2. Core questions (by theme)
3. Demographic/firmographic questions
4. Open-ended wrap-up
```

### Question Types

**Rating Scales (Likert)**
- Use 5 or 7 point scales
- Label all points, not just endpoints
- Keep scale direction consistent

**Multiple Choice**
- Include "Other" with text field
- Limit to 5-7 options
- Use "Select all that apply" sparingly

**Open-Ended**
- Use sparingly (1-3 per survey)
- Make optional when possible
- Place at end of sections

**Ranking**
- Limit to 5-7 items
- Only use when priority matters

### Survey Best Practices

**Length:**
- 5-10 minutes ideal
- 15 minutes maximum
- Show progress indicator

**Question Writing:**
- One concept per question
- Avoid leading questions
- Use simple language
- Be specific about timeframes

**Order:**
- Easy/engaging questions first
- Sensitive questions in middle
- Demographics at end

### Common Survey Types

**NPS Survey:**
```
Q1: On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend
    [Product] to a friend or colleague?

Q2: What's the primary reason for your score?
```

**Customer Satisfaction (CSAT):**
```
Q1: How satisfied are you with [specific experience]?
    - Very dissatisfied
    - Dissatisfied
    - Neutral
    - Satisfied
    - Very satisfied

Q2: What could we do to improve?
```

**Product-Market Fit (Sean Ellis):**
```
Q1: How would you feel if you could no longer use [Product]?
    - Very disappointed
    - Somewhat disappointed
    - Not disappointed
    - N/A - I no longer use [Product]

Q2: What type of people do you think would benefit most
    from [Product]?

Q3: What is the main benefit you receive from [Product]?

Q4: How can we improve [Product] for you?
```

## Persona Framework

### Persona Structure

```markdown
# [Persona Name]: [Descriptive Title]

## Demographics
- **Role:** [Job title]
- **Company:** [Size, stage, industry]
- **Reports to:** [Manager level]
- **Team:** [Who they manage/work with]

## Background
[2-3 sentences about their career path and current situation]

## Goals
1. [Primary goal]
2. [Secondary goal]
3. [Tertiary goal]

## Challenges
1. [Primary pain point]
2. [Secondary pain point]
3. [Tertiary pain point]

## Day in the Life
[Description of typical workday and how your product fits]

## Decision-Making
- **Buying role:** [Decision maker / Influencer / User]
- **Research behavior:** [How they find solutions]
- **Key criteria:** [What matters most in a purchase]
- **Objections:** [Common concerns]

## Quotes
> "[Actual quote from research that captures their perspective]"
> "[Another representative quote]"

## How We Help
[How your product specifically addresses their needs]

## Messaging Themes
- [Key message 1]
- [Key message 2]
- [Key message 3]
```

### Persona Types

**Buyer Persona:**
Focus on purchase decision, budget authority, evaluation criteria

**User Persona:**
Focus on daily workflows, feature usage, success metrics

**Negative Persona:**
Who is NOT your ideal customer (helps with targeting)

## Jobs-to-be-Done Framework

### JTBD Structure

```markdown
# Job: [Job Statement]

## Job Statement
When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [expected outcome].

## Functional Aspects
- What they need to accomplish
- Tasks and activities involved
- Tools and resources required

## Emotional Aspects
- How they want to feel
- Social/status considerations
- Fears and anxieties

## Circumstances
- When does this job arise?
- What triggers it?
- What context matters?

## Current Solutions
- What do they use today?
- What's the workaround?
- Why isn't it ideal?

## Success Criteria
- How do they know the job is done?
- What does "good" look like?
- How do they measure success?

## Related Jobs
- Jobs that happen before
- Jobs that happen after
- Competing jobs for attention
```

### Job Hierarchy

**Big Job (Life Goal)**
"Advance my career and be seen as a leader"

**Medium Job (Milestone)**
"Deliver projects on time and under budget"

**Small Job (Task)**
"Keep my team updated on project status"

## Voice-of-Customer Analysis

### Data Sources

**Direct Feedback:**
- Support tickets
- Sales call notes
- Customer interviews
- Survey responses
- Review sites (G2, Capterra)

**Indirect Signals:**
- Feature usage data
- Churn reasons
- Cancellation surveys
- Social media mentions

### Analysis Framework

**1. Collection**
- Gather raw feedback from all sources
- Tag by source, segment, topic
- Note frequency of mentions

**2. Categorization**
- Group by theme (pricing, features, support, etc.)
- Identify positive vs. negative sentiment
- Map to customer journey stage

**3. Quantification**
- Count frequency of themes
- Measure sentiment scores
- Track trends over time

**4. Synthesis**
- Identify top themes
- Extract representative quotes
- Note patterns by segment

**5. Action**
- Prioritize by impact and frequency
- Assign to teams (product, marketing, support)
- Track implementation and results

### VOC Template

```markdown
# Voice of Customer Analysis: [Topic/Timeframe]

## Summary
[2-3 sentence executive summary]

## Top Themes

### Theme 1: [Name] (X mentions)
**Sentiment:** Positive/Negative/Mixed
**Segments affected:** [Who]
**Representative quotes:**
- "[Quote 1]"
- "[Quote 2]"
**Recommended action:** [What to do]

### Theme 2: [Name] (X mentions)
...

## Patterns by Segment
[How feedback differs by segment]

## Trends Over Time
[What's changing]

## Recommendations
1. [Action 1]
2. [Action 2]
3. [Action 3]
```

## Research Ethics

### Consent
- Explain purpose clearly
- Get permission to record
- Allow opt-out at any time

### Privacy
- Anonymize data by default
- Store securely
- Share only with need-to-know

### Incentives
- Fair compensation for time
- Clear about what they get
- Fulfill promises promptly

### Representation
- Don't cherry-pick quotes
- Acknowledge limitations
- Present findings accurately
