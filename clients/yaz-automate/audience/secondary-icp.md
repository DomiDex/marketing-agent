# Secondary Ideal Customer Profiles

> Additional audiences — secondary buyers or adjacent markets.

## Secondary ICP #1: Operations Manager at Growing SMB

### At a Glance

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Job Title** | Operations Manager, Office Manager |
| **Department** | Operations |
| **Company Size** | 20-100 employees |
| **Industry** | Service businesses, agencies |
| **Relationship to Primary** | Influencer/End User |

### Who They Are

**Role description:**
> Responsible for keeping the business running smoothly day-to-day. Reports to the owner/CEO and manages processes, tools, and often a small team.

**How they differ from primary ICP:**
> Not the final decision maker, but has significant influence. More focused on operational efficiency than growth strategy. Often the one who will actually manage the AI solution.

### Pain Points

| Pain Point | Impact | Priority |
|------------|--------|----------|
| Processes that should be automated but aren't | Wasted time, errors | High |
| Too many manual handoffs between systems | Bottlenecks, delays | High |
| Difficulty scaling operations with current resources | Stress, overtime | Medium |

### Goals

| Goal | Metric | Priority |
|------|--------|----------|
| Streamline operations | Time saved, error reduction | High |
| Reduce manual work for team | Team satisfaction, efficiency | High |
| Look good to leadership | Recognition, promotions | Medium |

### Messaging Adjustments

**Lead with:** Operational efficiency and time savings

**Emphasize:** Ease of implementation, team adoption, measurable results

**Avoid:** High-level business strategy — they care about execution

### Real Quote

> "I need something that works reliably and doesn't create more work for my team"
> — Operations Manager perspective

## Secondary ICP #2: Tech-Curious Owner Looking to Modernize

### At a Glance

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Job Title** | Owner, Founder |
| **Department** | Leadership |
| **Company Size** | 10-50 employees |
| **Industry** | Traditional businesses going digital |
| **Relationship to Primary** | Buyer |

### Who They Are

**Role description:**
> Established business owner who recognizes the need to modernize but hasn't made the leap yet. Slightly older demographic, successful but worried about falling behind.

**How they differ from primary ICP:**
> Less tech-native, more skeptical, but also more willing to pay for done-for-you solutions. Values trust and relationships highly.

### Pain Points

| Pain Point | Impact | Priority |
|------------|--------|----------|
| Competitors seem more tech-savvy | Fear of falling behind | High |
| Don't know who to trust for tech help | Paralysis, bad past experiences | High |
| Worried about disrupting what works | Risk aversion | Medium |

### Goals

| Goal | Metric | Priority |
|------|--------|----------|
| Modernize without disruption | Smooth transition | High |
| Not get ripped off | Clear value for money | High |
| Understand what they're paying for | Plain English explanations | High |

### Messaging Adjustments

**Lead with:** Trust, plain English, done-for-you

**Emphasize:** We explain everything, no jargon, proven results

**Avoid:** Cutting-edge tech talk, assumptions about tech literacy

### Real Quote

> "I've had bad experiences with tech vendors who talked over my head and overcharged me"
> — Established owner perspective
