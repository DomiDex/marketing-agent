# Customer Journey

> How customers move from awareness to purchase — and what content helps at each stage.

---

## Journey Overview

```
[Awareness] → [Consideration] → [Decision] → [Onboarding] → [Success]
```

---

## Stage 1: Awareness

*They just realized they have a problem*

### Mindset

**What they're thinking:**
> "There has to be a better way to handle all this repetitive work. I keep hearing about AI — maybe that could help?"

**Emotional state:** Frustrated, Overwhelmed, Curious

**Level of urgency:** Medium — annoyed but not desperate yet

### Behavior

**What triggers this stage:**
- A particularly overwhelming week
- Reading about AI in business news
- Hearing a peer talk about automation
- Realizing competitors seem more efficient

**What they're searching for:**
| Search Query | Intent |
|--------------|--------|
| "How to automate my business" | Exploring options |
| "AI for small business" | Understanding what's possible |
| "Save time on repetitive tasks" | Looking for solutions |
| "Business automation ideas" | Seeking inspiration |

**Where they look:**
- Google
- LinkedIn
- Business podcasts
- YouTube

### Content That Helps

| Content Type | Purpose | Example Topics |
|--------------|---------|----------------|
| Blog posts | Educate on problem | "5 tasks every SMB should automate" |
| Social content | Build awareness | "How AI saved this agency 20 hours/week" |
| Videos | Explain concepts | "What is a business AI agent?" |

### Goals for This Stage

- [x] Help them understand and name their problem
- [x] Establish credibility as a trusted source
- [ ] Capture their attention/contact info

---

## Stage 2: Consideration

*They're comparing options*

### Mindset

**What they're thinking:**
> "Should I try to learn this myself, hire someone, or use a tool? What's the best option for someone like me?"

**Emotional state:** Analytical, Skeptical, Hopeful

**Level of urgency:** Medium-High

### Behavior

**What they're evaluating:**
- DIY tools (Zapier, Make, etc.)
- Hiring developers
- AI agencies (like us)
- Doing nothing

**What they're searching for:**
| Search Query | Intent |
|--------------|--------|
| "AI automation agency" | Looking for help |
| "Zapier vs hiring a developer" | Comparing options |
| "Is AI reliable for business?" | Addressing concerns |
| "Cost of business automation" | Understanding investment |

**Who they ask for input:**
- Other business owners
- Trusted advisors

### Content That Helps

| Content Type | Purpose | Example Topics |
|--------------|---------|----------------|
| Comparison guides | Help evaluate | "DIY vs Done-for-you automation" |
| Case studies | Prove results | "[Client] automated X and saved Y hours" |
| Webinars | Demonstrate value | "See how AI agents work" |
| FAQ | Address concerns | "Is AI reliable enough for my business?" |

### Goals for This Stage

- [x] Position as the best option for non-technical owners
- [x] Address key concerns and objections
- [ ] Move them toward a discovery call

---

## Stage 3: Decision

*They're ready to buy*

### Mindset

**What they're thinking:**
> "Can I trust these people? Will they understand my business? Is this worth the investment?"

**Emotional state:** Hopeful but Anxious

**Level of urgency:** High — ready to act

### Behavior

**What they need to see:**
- Clear process explanation
- Pricing transparency
- Social proof (testimonials, case studies)
- Risk mitigation (guarantees, support)

**What they're searching for:**
| Search Query | Intent |
|--------------|--------|
| "[Company] reviews" | Validating choice |
| "AI automation pricing" | Understanding cost |

**Who else is involved:**
- Partner/spouse (for smaller businesses)
- Maybe a key employee

### Content That Helps

| Content Type | Purpose | Example Topics |
|--------------|---------|----------------|
| Pricing page | Clarify investment | Clear packages/pricing |
| Process page | Show what to expect | "How we work" |
| Testimonials | Final validation | Video testimonials |
| Discovery call | Answer questions | Free consultation |

### Conversion Triggers

- Testimonial from similar business
- Clear ROI explanation
- Friendly, pressure-free sales call
- Quick win promise

### Deal Breakers

- Pushy sales tactics
- Vague pricing
- No testimonials or case studies
- Overly technical explanations

---

## Stage 4: Onboarding

*First 30 days as a customer*

### Mindset

**What they're thinking:**
> "I hope this works. I'm nervous but excited to see results."

**Emotional state:** Hopeful, Uncertain, Eager

### Behavior

**What they need to accomplish:**
| Milestone | Timeframe | Success Indicator |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Kick-off call | Day 1 | Clear understanding of process |
| First agent live | Week 2-3 | Working automation |
| First results | Week 3-4 | Time saved or leads captured |

**Common friction points:**
- Anxiety about whether it will work
- Impatience to see results
- Questions about how things work

### Content That Helps

| Content Type | Purpose | Timing |
|--------------|---------|--------|
| Welcome email | Orient and excite | Day 0-1 |
| Kick-off call | Align on goals | Day 1-2 |
| Progress updates | Build confidence | Weekly |
| Quick wins | Show value | Week 2-3 |

### Goals for This Stage

- [x] Get them to first "aha moment" quickly
- [x] Build confidence through communication
- [ ] Prevent buyer's remorse

---

## Stage 5: Success & Advocacy

*Getting value and potentially referring*

### Mindset

**What they're thinking:**
> "This is actually working. I have more time now. I should tell others about this."

### Expansion Opportunities

| Trigger | Opportunity | Approach |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| First agent success | Second automation project | "What else could we automate?" |
| Strong results | Case study | "Can we feature your success?" |
| Happy customer | Referral | "Know anyone else who'd benefit?" |

### Goals for This Stage

- [x] Expand account with additional projects
- [ ] Generate referrals and testimonials
- [ ] Turn customers into advocates
