# Competitors

> Competitive landscape — who Yaz Automate competes against.

---

## Competitive Overview

**Primary competitors:** DIY tools, other AI agencies, freelance developers

**Competitive position:** Niche player (new entrant)

**Key battleground:** SMB automation space

---

## Direct Competitors

### Competitor 1: Other AI Agencies

**Website:** Various

**Overview:**
> Agencies offering custom AI development and automation services. Range from small shops to larger consultancies.

**Target market:** Often enterprise-focused or tech-savvy businesses

**Pricing:** $5,000-$50,000+ per project

**Strengths:**
- Experienced teams
- Portfolio of work
- Can handle complex projects

**Weaknesses:**
- Often speak in technical jargon
- Enterprise focus = high prices
- May not understand SMB needs

**Their positioning:**
> "Custom AI development for businesses"

**How we beat them:**
> Focus on SMBs, plain English communication, right-sized pricing

**When we lose to them:**
> Complex enterprise requirements, need for large team

## Indirect Competitors

### Category 1: DIY Automation Platforms

**Examples:** Zapier, Make (Integromat), n8n, Bardeen

**When prospects choose them:** Want to DIY, very price-sensitive, tech-savvy

**How we compete:** "Those tools expect you to figure it out yourself. We do it for you."

### Category 2: Freelance Developers

**Examples:** Upwork, Fiverr, personal networks

**When prospects choose them:** Lower budget, have technical oversight ability

**How we compete:** "Freelancers hand off and disappear. We provide ongoing support."

### Category 3: In-house Development

**Examples:** Hiring a developer or tech person

**When prospects choose them:** Large enough to justify headcount, want full control

**How we compete:** "Hiring costs $60K+ per year before you get any results. We deliver in weeks for a fraction of that."

### Category 4: Manual Processes / Doing Nothing

**Examples:** Spreadsheets, manual work, status quo

**When prospects choose them:** Don't realize automation is possible, fear of change

**How we compete:** "You're spending X hours per week on this. What would you do with that time back?"

## Competitive Battlecards

### vs. DIY Tools (Zapier, Make)

**Quick differentiators:**
- We have: Done-for-you service, ongoing support
- They have: Lower cost, self-service
- Price comparison: Higher upfront, but no time investment from customer

**Key questions to ask prospects:**
1. "Have you tried these tools before? What happened?"
2. "How much time do you have to learn and maintain automation?"

**Landmines to plant:**
- "How much is your time worth? Is it worth 20 hours to save $2,000?"

### vs. Other Agencies

**Quick differentiators:**
- We have: SMB focus, plain English, right-sized pricing
- They have: Bigger teams, more portfolio
- Price comparison: Often significantly lower

**Key questions to ask prospects:**
1. "Did they explain things in a way you understood?"
2. "What's their ongoing support model?"
